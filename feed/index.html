]]> Gantt charts are like the hit Korean drama Squid Game–people either love it or hate it (because they don’t get it). Moreover, research indicates that Gantt Charts are one of the most-used planning and controlling tools in projects today.

According to a survey done with 750 project managers, the Gantt Chart was the fourth most-used tool (out of 70 tools and techniques) associated with project management.

No matter which side of the spectrum you are on, seasoned project managers understand the importance of using Gantt charts in their day-to-day work. In this guide, we will simplify the mystery of Gantt charts and learn how project managers can use Gantt scheduling software to up their project management game.

What is a Gantt chart?

Most people confuse a Gantt chart with a project management plan and wrongly do so. Having been developed over a hundred years ago, the Gantt chart tool has proved its resiliency over time.

A Gantt chart is basically a bar chart, which is used for team reporting and helps understand the kind of tasks that you need to do to complete a project successfully. It includes details about planned activities as well as dates, project progress, activity names, activity duration, activity start and finish dates, and more.

One of the most important distinctions between a Gantt chart and a project management plan is that a Gantt chart is useful for progress tracking and reporting; it is not useful for any project planning or organizing.

You should use a robust Gantt chart project management software to make the most of your Gantt chart.

What is a Gantt Chart Project Management Software?

A Gantt chart software is a widely used project management tool that demonstrates what needs to be done and when in an easy-to-understand visual format. Whether you want to monitor the project schedule or get insights into the interdependencies between tasks, a Gantt chart software comes in handy.

When using a Gantt chart, remember that one task must be completed before moving on to the next.

When to Use a Free Online Gantt Chart Maker?

With the basics underway, let’s address the most important question you may have: “When to use a free Gantt chart maker online?”

You should use a Gantt chart maker online when:

  • You are driving a project that requires multiple people to perform a variety of tasks
  • You want an overview of the project’s key milestones, deadlines, completed tasks, and project roadmap
  • You want to drive large-scale projects with greater efficiency
  • You want important details about the project, such as project start and finish dates for each task, roles and responsibilities for each member performing the task, dependencies of tasks, resources needed for each task, the time needed for each task, and how the tasks are related to each other

The takeaway: Think of a Gantt chart as a guiding map for your team members to follow throughout the project. 

360-degree Advantages of a Gantt Chart Software

In this section, we will look at what defines as the best Gantt chart software. We will also look at the benefits of using a Gantt chart online maker–whether you use it in the form of a free Excel Gantt chart template or a full-fledged Gantt chart software.

A Gantt chart software is a multi-purpose tool that helps project managers to:

Optimize the Workflow and Automate Processing

An optimized project workflow makes teamwork more transparent, productive, and friction-free. It also makes collaboration among teams (both remote and on-site) more easy and more fun, ensuring everyone is on the same page at all times.

Moreover, team members can stay laser-focused on the tasks while working with greater accountability for their individual tasks.

This tool allows project managers to set up their projects in no time at all, as they can track the project progress as well as status with a single glance.

They can also manage multiple projects at a time without any chaos or confusion impeding the project’s progress.

The project managers can use Gantt charts to toggle between tasks at the click of a button. Using the board view, they can:

  • Manage every little detail of the task
  • Invite key internal and external stakeholders to collaborate on a project
  • Share the project plan with others in real-time and set filters as needed
  • Attach important files and get notifications as well as leave comments, ensuring seamless teamwork

Get a High-Level Overview of The Project Progress and Status

Gantt charts are the doorway into how your project is faring. Whether you want to understand the timelines for each task and how every task relates to each other, or you wish to indicate how a task can get initiated only after related tasks are completed.

This bird’s eye view of the project activities drives informed decision-making among managers and allows them to course-correct while eliminating project hurdles.

Allocate Resources Optimally

As per PMI, lack of resources is one of the primary reasons for project failure. This is where a Gantt chart software can help project managers to manage their resources more optimally as it illustrates task overlaps and dependencies.

They can also set realistic deadlines for the project and assign resources accordingly, ensuring that the right team members are working on the right tasks at all times.

More importantly, project managers can quickly reassign resources in instances of unpredictable issues cropping up.

Track Key Elements Within a Project

A Gantt chart software is essentially a robust tracking tool–from tracking tasks and constraints to monitoring the workflow and key performance indicators, it can help managers to stay on top of their projects without breaking a sweat.

Here’s a handy cheat sheet for you when making a Gantt chart. Use these expert-approved tips to elevate your project management expertise:

Disadvantages of Using a Gantt Chart Software

Now that we have a good understanding of the benefits of using a Gantt chart software, let’s look at its limitations:

  • A Gantt chart software has a learning curve, and using it for beginners can be challenging as well as time-consuming.
  • With large-scale projects and numerous tasks, sub-tasks, and activities, the Gantt chart can become convoluted and complicated to understand and lose its basic essence.
  • A Gantt chart software does not solely focus on showcasing the task ‘priority,’ which can make it confusing for team members working on a project.
  • A Gantt chart does not throw detailed insights into task dependencies. It will not elaborate on intricate details that revolve around dependent tasks. This is why it is recommended that project managers use the (PERT) chart in sync with the Gantt chart.

In Summary

Among a sea of different project management softwares, Gantt chart softwares are extremely useful. For many project managers, Gantt charts have emerged as a ‘shared language’ that brings the team together and ensures a great track record of having delivered winning projects, time after time.

So, What is Visual Project Management (VPM)?

According to PMI, “Visual project management is a new practice concept that integrates visual thinking tools and data visualization methodologies with more traditional project communication, reporting, and facilitation practices.”

In other words, visual project management, or VPM as it is commonly known, is a type of ‘lean’ project management methodology. It empowers project managers to:

  • Achieve project tasks and milestones efficiently and effectively within the proposed timeline
  • Gain insights into key project deliverables and boost visibility
  • Engage in visual thinking and leverage tools
  • Improve the project performance and effectiveness

]]> Gantt charts are every project manager’s best friend (and understandably so). The term is often thrown around a lot at project kickoff meetings or when there’s talk of boosting productivity and efficiency.

Gantt charts empower project managers to visualize time and resources within a project as simply and comprehensively as possible. All you need to do is, factor in the task duration and dependencies (if any), and voila, you’ve got a Gantt chart at hand.

What is a Gantt chart?

Gantt chart is named after Henry Gantt, who popularized its use in the early 20th century.

Visually, a Gantt chart is represented with horizontal bars. The chart illustrates a project’s timeline and dates, task owner, project schedule, upcoming milestones, and associated tasks/events during the project lifecycle.

Each bar represents a task, and the length of the bar represents the time it will take to complete said task. In essence, a bird’s eye view of the chart will offer important at-a-glance insights into:

  • What work needs to be done, including the project tasks and events
  • Who needs to be doing it, and how does each member’s work impacts the overall project progress
  • When it needs to be done, including the start and end dates of the project
  • How the project tasks connect as well as overlap with each other and how they should be grouped
  • What important milestones and deadlines are around the corner
  • The percentage completed for each activity

Note: Gantt charts use ‘critical paths’ – a methodology that helps mitigate risks and scenarios where individual tasks could cause unforeseen delays in the subsequent tasks, ultimately derailing the whole project. As the name suggests, the ‘critical’ path allows you to understand which tasks to undertake first to be able to complete the entire project on time and successfully.

So What Is a Gantt Scheduling Software?

Originally, Gantt charts were drawn by hand on a piece of paper. This meant that every time there was a change in the plan, it needed to be redrawn – a huge inconvenience.

Today, project managers can make a Gantt chart online using Gantt chart software. Also known as a project management software, the Gantt chart was built to automate the project planning process.

The software also supports more advanced features such as:

  • Creating task dependencies such as start-to-start, finish-to-start, finish-to-finish, and start-to-finish
  • Adding milestones and project phases
  • Identifying the critical path of a project plan
  • Creating a work breakdown structure
  • Assign tasks to the team and track project progress in real-time
  • Dragging and dropping timelines to update your project schedule instantly

These features enabled project managers to save valuable time and effort. Additionally, there are free online Gantt charts that project managers can leverage online and share with peers to drive a more collaborative project management process.

Project managers can use project management tools that are equipped with a Gantt chart maker online feature. Note that the project management tool should support the format offered by the free Gantt chart maker online for it to work.

You can also use a free Excel Gantt chart template (as Microsoft Excel itself doesn’t allow you to make Gantt charts) instead of a project management tool.

Top-7 Benefits of Gantt Chart Project Management Software

A Gantt chart software offers multiple benefits to users and organizations at large:

  • It allows you to map tasks (and, by extension, projects) in a hierarchical and incremental fashion, create accurate timelines, and keep the deliverables on track.
  • It helps you to track the project schedule and engage in an informed project planning process.
  • It allows you to create dynamic online Gantt charts collaboratively with your team. This includes key tasks such as creating timelines, dependencies, etc., and adding milestones. You can also track costs and keep tabs on the budget. The best part? As the project progresses, the Gantt chart will automatically get updated.
  • If you have too many task dependencies, using a Gantt chart can help you understand which tasks to undertake and in which order.
  • It allows you to prioritize important tasks and focus on what truly matters.
  • It enables you to monitor project progress and stay on top of your deliveries.
  • It helps make the project easier to digest as the tasks are broken down into smaller, more realistic, and achievable chunks so Gantt charts are the way to go if you need to build and manage complex projects.

Six Characteristics of the Best Gantt Chart Software Out There

Your Gantt chart software should have the following six features allowing for seamless project planning:

  • Milestones: It should allow you to represent the project milestones, which basically mark the end of a task series or a project phase.
  • Dependencies: It should throw light on the task dependencies, which demonstrate which tasks are linked together and these ‘links’ are shown by lines, and the arrows indicate which tasks are to be done next.
  • Task summary: It should provide a summary of the tasks so you can group them together as part of a project phase; it should also give a glimpse of the task progress to ensure everyone is on the same page.
  • Collaboration: It should allow project managers to collaborate with the team online and in real-time, or else your Gantt chart becomes static in nature. It should provide chat and comment features and should help keep the workflow documented from start to finish. Finally, it should provide instant alerts and notifications every time a task is updated or completed so that everyone is in the know.
  • File-sharing: It should provide useful file-sharing capabilities, which include sharing images, documents, videos, notes, and so on at the click of a button. This feature allows the Gantt chart tool to double up as a centralized place for all things project-related, empowering project managers to keep all the task and project information together.
  • Actual vs. planned progress: It should provide details on the actual versus planned progress. This feature makes an online Gantt chart software better than static charts. All project managers need to do is set a baseline at the start of the project to get invaluable insights into how the project is progressing versus how it should progress. If you want an optimized tracking experience, this last feature is essential.

How Do You Create a Gantt Chart with A Software?

Now that we have covered the basics of Gantt chart software, let’s understand the steps to create a Gantt chart online:

  • Step 1: Start by making a list of the tasks you need to be able to finish the project.
  • Step 2: Next, you’ll need to define the first and last dates for every task. Make sure to consult your team for this step. Also, factor in additional follow-up tasks you may need to drive past the end date.
  • Step 3: Then, you’ll need to create a timeline for the project based on the task duration.
  • Step 4: In this step, you’ll need to identify the task dependencies.
  • Step 5: This step requires you to fill out the bar chart timeline with tasks.
  • Step 6: This crucial step entails assigning tasks to the team members based on their availability.
  • Step 7: In this step, you assign the key milestones for your project, such as conducting meetings, getting project approvals, task starting points, mid-phase check-ins, and more.
  • Step 8: Finally, you’ll need to work towards identifying the critical path.

Pro tip: Keep modifying your Gantt chart as the project plan changes. For this, you’ll need a tool that automatically updates task/event dependencies in real-time.

Gantt Chart Tips to Keep in Mind

In the final section, let’s run through a few tips to keep in mind to help you make the most out of your Gantt charts software:

  • Tip 1: Make the Gantt chart project planning process a collaborative effort. You need to ensure that your team is involved at every stage of decision-making and planning to keep everyone in the loop at all times.
  • Tip 2: Make sure to customize the Gantt chart as per your team’s needs. For instance, you can use different colors for different team members when assigning tasks. The idea is to make the Gantt chart your own and see what works for you.
  • Tip 3: Make sure to leverage the collaboration features. This includes attaching important files, notes, images, and so on. The goal is to make the plan relevant, updated, and easily accessible by one-and-all.
  • Tip 4: Ensure that your team gets email alerts as the plan changes dynamically. Your team members should be alerted whenever a task gets completed or changed.

Final Thoughts

All in all, a Gantt chart is a project management tool that is intrinsically visual in nature. It empowers project managers to get a real-time overview of the project’s progress and status. This dynamic project management tool includes critical components of a project such as project tasks, duration, milestones, dependent tasks, and so on. Gantt charts online can be used by anyone who wishes to visualize their project planning process in a simple, in-depth, and meaningful manner.
